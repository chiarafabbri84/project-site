Abstract


Welcome to our KE4H project, an exploration and enrichment of the entities representing Rimini’s cultural landmarks through the framework of the Wikidata Knowledge Graph. Although Wikidata is one of the most extensive open knowledge bases, we found that several important monuments in Rimini, including the Arch of Augustus, the Malatesta Temple, the Tiberius Bridge, the Sant’Agostino's church, and the Domus del Chirurgo, lacked fundamental information. Basic facts such as architectural style, building materials, inscriptions, or official websites were often missing or incomplete.

To bridge these semantic gaps, we leveraged a combination of technologies and tools. Using SPARQL queries, we analyzed the current state of the data on Wikidata. Then, with the help of Large Language Models (LLMs) like Chat GPT, Deep Seek and Gemini, we retrieved and generated missing RDF triples, following Wikidata’s ontology. All model-generated content was manually corrected in order to have final well-constructed triples that can be potentially added to the entities.

The project is organized as a website built using Notepad++ and GitHub Pages, and it’s divided into several sections: Home, Methodology, SPARQL Queries, Monuments, Faced Challenges, and Conclusions.
