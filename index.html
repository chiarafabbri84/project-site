PALAZZO DEI DIAMANTI
ArCo semantic enrichment




Introduction


This project was carried out as part of the KE4H (Knowledge Engineering for the Humanities) course at the University of Bologna. For this purpose, we used ArCo as our foundational knowledge graph. ArCo is the official ontology network and RDF dataset developed by the Italian Ministry of Culture. We found that the cultural knowledge graph offers a rich and well-organized representation of Italy’s cultural heritage, supported by a SPARQL endpoint that enables complex semantic querying, but it presents some gaps.

The case study chosen for our project was the Palazzo dei Diamanti. Constructed in the late 15th century and attributed to the architect Biagio Rossetti, the building is famous for its distinctive diamond-shaped exterior walls, which give the palace its name.



Why did we choose Palazzo dei Diamanti?


Palazzo dei Diamanti is a key monument in Ferrara and a fundamental example of Renaissance architecture. The monument is part of the Addizione Erculea an urban expansion project carried out in Ferrara at the end of the 15th century, initiated by Duke Ercole I d’Este, during the rule of the Este court. This expansion secured a place for Ferrara in the UNESCO World Heritage List. The Palazzo dei Diamanti was commissioned by Sigismondo d’Este and served as the residence of several members of the Este family. Today, the Palazzo dei Diamanti functions as an important cultural institution, hosting the Pinacoteca Nazionale and major temporary exhibitions, yet it remains underrepresented in many structured digital resources. This made it an ideal subject for knowledge graph enrichment through semantic technologies, especially considering that one of our team members is originally from Ferrara.



Objectives


  • To fill the semantic gaps we were able to detect within ArCo
  • To better our abilities in the use of Large Language Models (LLMs), particularly ChatGPT and DeepSeek, in order to create RDF triples that would add meaningful and semantically accurate information to the ArCo knowledge graph
